Imran Khan

5 oct 1952

Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf

Imran Ahmed Khan Niazi is a Pakistani politician, philanthropist, and former cricketer who served as the 19th prime minister of Pakistan from August 2018 until April 2022. He is the founder of the political party Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf and was its chairman from 1996 to 2023.

Biography

  • Born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan.
  • Educated at the Royal Grammar School in Worcester and Keble College, Oxford.
  • Started his cricket career in 1971 and captained the Pakistan national team.
  • Led Pakistan to its first and only Cricket World Cup victory in 1992.
  • Retired from cricket and entered politics, founding the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf party in 1996.
  • Served as Prime Minister of Pakistan from August 2018 until April 2022.
